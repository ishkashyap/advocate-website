## COMMERCIAL COURTS ACT, 2015 — KEY POINTS

1. **Specified value** = principal + interest + damages + penalties (Section 2(1)(i))
2. **Threshold**: ₹3,00,000 (amended from ₹1,00,000 in 2018)
3. **Fee**: Same as regular state-wise court fee on specified value
4. **No separate commercial court fee schedule** in any state
5. **Arbitration matters**: Sections 9, 11, 34, 37 — fixed fee ₹650 under Schedule II
6. **Appeals**: Commercial appeal fee same as original suit fee

## ASSUMPTIONS & DISCLAIMERS

1. **Accuracy**: Slab rates are based on the respective Court Fees Acts and amendments as of 2024. State-specific amendments may alter rates in specific districts or for specific case types.
2. **Commercial Courts**: No state has enacted a separate commercial court fee schedule. All states apply regular court fee rates to the "specified value" under the Commercial Courts Act 2015.
3. **Fee Caps**: Maximum fee caps exist in most states but at differing amounts. Some caps were introduced via state amendments and may not apply to all case types.
4. **Schedule II Fixed Fees**: The fixed fees (₹150/₹100/₹500/₹650) are standard across states but should be verified against the latest state Schedule II notifications.
5. **Rounding**: All states round up (ceil) to the nearest rupee. No state allows fractional court fees.
6. **Probate/Succession**: The 2.5% rate is standard under Section 19-H but some states have slab-based probate fees.
7. **Consumer Complaints**: Fees under the Consumer Protection Act 2019 follow a separate slab structure unrelated to the Court Fees Act.
8. **This data is for informational purposes**. Users should verify with the latest state notifications before filing.
