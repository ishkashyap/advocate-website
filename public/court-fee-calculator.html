Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about court fees in India.

How is court fee calculated for a money suit?

For money suits, court fee is calculated based on the claimed amount (ad valorem). The rate varies by state, typically ranging from 1% to 7.5% of the suit value, applied in different slabs. Each state has its own Court Fees Act with specific slab rates.

Is court fee refundable?

Generally, court fees are non-refundable. However, partial refund may be available if a suit is withdrawn before the first hearing or settled through mediation, depending on state-specific rules.

Do different states have different court fee structures?

Yes, each Indian state has its own court fee structure based on the central Court Fees Act, 1870 with state-specific amendments. The slab rates, minimum fees, and maximum caps vary significantly from state to state.

What is the court fee for a writ petition?

Writ petitions typically have a fixed court fee that is not based on suit value. The amount varies by state but is usually between ₹50 to ₹500. For example, Delhi High Court charges ₹50 for a writ petition.

How to pay court fees in India?

Court fees can be paid through court fee stamps (available at court complexes), e-payment through the court's online portal, demand drafts, or challan at designated banks. Many courts now offer online payment facilities.

What is the court fee for filing an appeal?

For civil appeals, the court fee is typically half of the original suit court fee, subject to a minimum fee. Some states have specific rules for different types of appeals. Second appeals usually require full court fee.
